What are Graph Neural Networks?

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are deep learning architectures designed to work directly with graph-structured data. They have revolutionized social network analysis, recommendation systems, knowledge graphs, and many other domains by learning representations that respect the relational structure of data.

Key Concepts

Nodes & Edges

Graphs consist of nodes (vertices) representing entities and edges representing relationships between them. Nodes could be users, items, or any entities.

Node Features

Each node has associated features that describe its properties. These features are transformed through message passing to create richer representations.

Message Passing

Nodes aggregate information from their neighbors, then update their own representations. This process is repeated across multiple layers to propagate information.

Graph Readout

After message passing, node representations are aggregated into a graph-level representation for tasks like classification or regression.

Message Passing: The Core Operation

Message Passing Framework:

For each layer $k$, each node $i$ performs:

Step 1 - Aggregate: Collect messages from neighbors

$$m_i^{(k)} = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} \text{MSG}(h_j^{(k-1)})$$

Step 2 - Update: Combine with own representation

$$h_i^{(k)} = \text{UPDATE}(h_i^{(k-1)}, m_i^{(k)})$$

Why GNNs?

Many real-world problems have natural graph structures: social networks, citation networks, knowledge graphs, and biological networks. GNNs can learn powerful representations directly from these structures without hand-crafted features, respecting the graph's topology and capturing complex relationships.

GNN Applications

Graph Neural Networks are used across many domains. Here are some key application areas and how GNNs are applied in each.

Social Network Analysis

Predict user behavior, detect communities, recommend connections. Nodes represent users, edges represent friendships or interactions.

Recommendation Systems

Users and items form a bipartite graph. GNNs learn user preferences and item characteristics through collaborative filtering.

Knowledge Graphs

Reason over structured knowledge. Complete missing facts, answer queries, discover new relationships between entities.

Traffic Prediction

Road networks are spatial graphs. GNNs predict traffic flow by considering spatial dependencies between road segments.

Citation Networks

Papers cite other papers forming a graph. GNNs classify papers, predict citations, and discover research trends.

Fraud Detection

Transactions and users form graphs. GNNs detect anomalous patterns that indicate fraudulent activity.

Popular GNN Architectures

GCN (Graph Convolutional Network)

Spectral approach. Normalizes adjacency matrix and applies linear transformation. Simple and effective for many tasks.

GAT (Graph Attention Network)

Uses attention mechanism to weight neighbor contributions differently. Learns which neighbors are most relevant.

GraphSAGE

Inductive learning approach. Samples neighbors and aggregates using mean, LSTM, or pooling. Scales to large graphs.

Message Passing Neural Networks

General framework encompassing many GNN variants. Flexible message and update functions.

Choosing the Right Architecture

The choice depends on your data and task. GCN is a good starting point. GAT is useful when neighbors have varying importance. GraphSAGE scales to large graphs and supports inductive learning on unseen nodes.
